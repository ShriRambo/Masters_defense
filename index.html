Model Description

Model Description


Energy Distribution

Interpretation of Energy Density


\begin{align} E(\mathbf{x},t) = E_{0}(t).\varphi(\mathbf{x},t) &= E_{0}(t).\varphi(\mathbf{x}_p,t).\varphi(\mathbf{x}_s,t|\mathbf{x}_p) \\ \\ \mbox{where} \ \ \mathbf{x}_p = [x,y]^T, \ & \ \mathbf{x}_s = [\theta,\omega]^T \end{align}

Generalized Setting

\begin{align} \mathbf{x}= [x,y,\theta,\omega]^T, \quad \mathbf{C} = [C_x,C_y,C_\theta,C_\omega]^T \end{align}

Domain: $R \subseteq \mathbb{R}^4$, Boundary: $\partial R$


Velocity field $\mathbf{C}$ is derived from Shoaling and Reffraction models

Equations to be solved


\begin{align} \dfrac{\partial E(\mathbf{x},t)}{\partial t} + \nabla.(E(\mathbf{x},&t)\mathbf{C}(\mathbf{x},t)) = S(\mathbf{x},t) \\ &\Downarrow \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial t}p(\textbf{x},t) + \nabla.&(p(\textbf{x},t)\mathbf{C}) = 0. \end{align}


$p(\textbf{x},t)\propto E(\mathbf{x},t)$ $\ \ \ \leftrightarrow \ \ \ S(\mathbf{x},t) = 0$

Accommodating Source Term

\begin{align} E(\mathbf{x},t) = E_h(\mathbf{x},t)w(\mathbf{x},t) \\ \end{align}

\begin{align} &\quad\quad\downarrow \\ \frac{d e}{d t} = e&\frac{S(\textbf{x},t)}{E(\textbf{x},t)} = e\lambda(\mathbf{x},t) \end{align}

\begin{align} &\downarrow \\ e(t) =& e_0\exp\left(\lambda t\right) \end{align}

Multiple source terms

\begin{align*} S_{tot}(\textbf{x},t) &= S_{1}(\textbf{x},t) + S_{2}(\textbf{x},t)\dots \\ \implies \lambda_{tot} &= \lambda_{1} +\lambda_{2}\dots \\ \implies e(t) &= e_0\exp(\lambda_{1} t)\exp(\lambda_{2}t)\dots \end{align*}

Bottom friction

\begin{align} \lambda_{fric} = \frac{ S_{fric}(\textbf{x},t)}{E(\textbf{x},t) } = -C_b \frac{\omega^2}{g^2\sinh^2(kd)} \end{align}

Depth-induced wave breaking


\begin{align} \lambda_{br} = \frac{ S_{br}(\textbf{x},t)}{E(\textbf{x},t) } = -\frac{\alpha\rho g \big[ Q_0\bar{f}_0 H_{max}^2 \big]_{\textbf{x}_p,t}}{4E(\textbf{x}_p,t)} \end{align}

The Algorithm


  • Discretize time in $t_1,t_2,\dots$

  • For $i = 1,2,\dots$ do:
    • Sample $\mathbf{x}$ from $E(\mathbf{x},t_i)|_{\partial R}$
    • Assign energy to each particle
    • Propagate every particle by one step
    		•  $\Bigg\}$ Particle path computation

  • Estimate $E_h(\mathbf{x},t)$

  • For each iteration do:
    • Update $S(\mathbf{x},t)$
    • Update $e$
    • Estimate $E(\mathbf{x},t_i)$
    		•  $\Bigg\}$ Picard's Iteration

Conclusions

Conclusions

  • Implementation
    • Energy density as probability density
    • Energy balance as Fokker-Planck equation
    • Wave energy as particles
    • Iterative approach for source terms
  • Accuracy of the solution
    • At par with SWAN and analytical solution
    • Convergence of iterative scheme
    • Monochromatic as well as wave spectrum
    • Small error in shallower region
  • Computational Cost
    • Comparable with SWAN
    • Depends on the bathymetry
    • Depends on number of particles
    • Scope of numerical optimization
  • Additional Processes
    • Non-linear wave interactions
    • Diffraction
